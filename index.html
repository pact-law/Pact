// Core Lessons
§ Section 2(a) — Offer & Acceptance

A contract begins the moment one party makes a clear offer and the other accepts it — no paperwork required. A WhatsApp "yes, deal" can be legally binding. The offer must be communicated; silence is not acceptance.

§ Section 2(d) — Consideration

Every contract needs something exchanged — money, services, goods, or even a promise. "I'll build your site for RM1,000" — the RM1,000 is the consideration. No consideration = no enforceable contract.

§ Section 11 — Capacity (The Minor Problem)

Under Malaysian law, contracts with minors (under 18) are void — not just voidable, void. This protects you, but also means you can't enforce a contract you sign as a minor. Exception: necessities like food and shelter.

§ Section 24 — Void vs Voidable

Void = never existed. Voidable = valid until you cancel it. Fraud, duress, and misrepresentation make a contract voidable — you have the right to walk away. Know the difference before you sign anything.

§ Section 74 — Breach & Remedies

If someone breaks the contract, you can claim damages — but only for losses that were reasonably foreseeable. You can't claim RM50,000 if the contract was for RM500. Courts award fair compensation, not revenge money.
